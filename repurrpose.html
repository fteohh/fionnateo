Repurrpose
Improve NU Challenge | Spring 2020


This project was created for Northwestern University's Improve NU Challenge, a pitch competition that provides students with funding to implement their ideas with the goal of improving student experience. We placed second in the competition and are set to recieve $3,000 to implement our program across campus.

My inital project was created by my roommate, McKenna Lanter and I, titled Repurrposed.

After the first round, we consulted with the panelists and combined groups with Anna Yang and Karina Aguilar, whose goals of sustainability and providing low-barrier resources aligned with ours. The project was later renamed Repurrpose.

Problem: During move-out, students donate/get-rid of thousands of pounds of good, usable items that incoming students and students moving off campus could use.

Goal: Reduce resource excess, promote sustainability, and provide affordable access to students.

Solution:

  1. Place community bins in every dorm halls for small and large scale exchanges
  2. Host a donation drive at the end of every quarter
  3. Create a digital platform for students to use
  4. Exchange items for free or extremely low prices
  5. Partner with existing infrastructure such as the NU Office of Sustainability, Student Enrichment Services (SES), and NU Thrift Store

Takeaways:

This project started out as a fleeting idea scribbed on a sticky note. I had a great time with this challenge and am excited to see this idea come to life to improve something within my school that I'm passionate about.
